# Product Blueprint Reference Deconstruction

Use this after `product-blueprint:research`. Screenshots are raw evidence; deconstruction turns them into product decisions.

## Deconstruction Passes

1. **User job**: What does the user believe they are doing?
2. **Entry gate**: What must happen before the user reaches the valuable action?
3. **Screen role**: What is each screen allowed to do, and what must it not do?
4. **Decision points**: What must the user choose, inspect, confirm, or pay for?
5. **State model**: New, returning, empty, locked, paid, unsafe, loading, failure, success, replay.
6. **Mechanisms**: Memory, judging, ranking, recommendation, moderation, cost, creator publishing.
7. **Trust surfaces**: What explains why something happened, how to recover, and how to control it?
8. **Design language**: What visual choices support the product loop, not just the brand mood?
9. **Copyable principles**: Abstract the principle. Do not copy component styling unless it is generic.
10. **Do-not-copy list**: Identify UI, copy, flow, or business rules that should not be replicated.

## Rules

- Do not summarize screenshots as a feature list.
- Do not copy the reference product's brand, layout, or content.
- Separate `Observed`, `Inferred`, and `Opportunity`.
- When a reference flow is incomplete, mark the unknown instead of filling it with imagination.
- Translate every useful reference detail into a product principle or screen contract candidate.

## Output

Create `01.5-reference-deconstruction.md` with:

- Reference evidence map
- Feature-to-mental-model table
- Screen role and entry-gate map
- Mechanism and trust-surface map
- Product principles to reuse
- Anti-copy list
- Open research gaps
